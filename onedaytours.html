The Wolf Conservation Center’s mission is to promote wolf conservation by teaching about wolves, their relationship to the environment, and the human role in protecting their future. Our visit begins with a brief educational power point presentation in the cozy cabin classroom. The second portion of the program occurs outdoors by the wolf enclosures. Our guide answers guests’ questions as we observe wolf behaviour and we howl with the wolves. We will see the ambassador wolves and Mexican wolves. The center started in 1996 is located in South Salem, NY. Then we will visit the Keeler Tavern Museum, listed on the National Register of Historic Places since 1982, embodies the life of the community from the early 18th century through the mid-20th century. The Keeler Tavern has been a farmhouse, tavern, stagecoach stop, post office, hotel, home of noted architect Cass Gilbert and, since 1966, a museum.
